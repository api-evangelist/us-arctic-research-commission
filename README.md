# US Arctic Research Commission

The US Arctic Research Commission (USARC) is an independent federal agency that provides recommendations and advice to the President and Congress on Arctic research and policy. The Commission establishes national policy, priorities, and goals for federal Arctic scientific research, advises the Interagency Arctic Research Policy Committee (IARPC), and coordinates with Arctic residents, international programs, and local institutions. Key focus areas include climate change, ecosystems, human health, Indigenous Knowledge, economic resilience, critical minerals, and international Arctic cooperation.
